Mainnet Launch

The First Based Rollup

Remember May 27, 2024? That's the day we made history by launching the first based rollup on Ethereum mainnet, marking a strong advancement in the rollup-centric roadmap.

In a memorable moment, Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin proposed Taiko's genesis block, noting: "I'm excited to see that Taiko is launching as a based rollup. Ethereum benefits from L2s taking a plurality of different approaches, and I appreciate them being among the first to go in this direction.”

This breakthrough represents a technical achievement for Taiko and a fundamental shift in Ethereum's scaling paradigm. As a based rollup, we've demonstrated how to effectively scale Ethereum while preserving its core principles of decentralization and security.

Technical Achievement

ZK Proof Integration

We successfully integrated ZK proofs into the protocol while maintaining SGX proof support, strengthening our security architecture and building a multi-proof standard. This hybrid approach combines the speed of SGX proofs with the mathematical guarantees of ZK proofs.

BCR Optimization

In October, Ontake - our first hardfork (the v1.10.0 upgrade) - enhanced L2 EIP-1559 implementation by making base fees L1-tunable and introduced initial support for transaction preconfirmation. This upgrade significantly improved block proposer efficiency and network scalability.

Gwyneth Development

We made substantial progress on Taiko Gwyneth, our synchronously composable booster rollup designed to extend L1 capabilities through native sequencing and seamless L1-L2 cross-chain state access. This new rollup design will fully take advantage of being a based rollup, and work towards the end goal of a scalable L1.

What’s Next?

As we move into 2025, several key initiatives are underway to take Taiko’s growth even further:

Preconfirmations

Taiko has longer block times due to its based rollup nature. Based preconfirmations are our top priority to deliver sub-second user experiences on Taiko. We're working closely with our partners to develop this feature, aiming to implement the first preconfirmation protocol in early 2025.

ZK Proofs

Currently, 3 out of every 100 blocks proposed by Taiko Labs requires ZK proofs. In 2025, ZK requirements will apply to all proposers, with a target of 3% ZK coverage. Our goal is to gradually transition to a complete ZK-Rollup by the end of 2025.

DAO

Decentralization has been Taiko's backbone from day one. We're excited to invite our community to participate in Taiko's decision-making process. With the DAO and Security Council design expected to be finalised soon, we look forward to launching these governance structures this year.

Gwyneth

While Taiko pioneered based rollups and opened many opportunities, we believe in pushing boundaries further. This vision led us to build Gwyneth, a new based booster rollup initiative. Through the upcoming Gwyneth devnet/testnet in 2025, we aim to address the fragmentation and interoperability challenges in the rollup-centric roadmap.
